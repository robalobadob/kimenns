About Me

Kim Enns is a certified Master Gardener and has been working in agriculture and urban farms since 2012. She has had experience working on everything from 20 acre organic vegetable farms to productive rooftop gardens, and she now uses that knowledge to lead workshops or offer one on one consultation and coaching.

Kim aims for a simple, informative and light hearted approach to help you with whatever your garden goals are, whether you want to start your own mud kitchen and bug hotel, or teach kids how to plant and cook their own veggies, or make your current garden more pollinator friendly.

Contact Kim to help get your garden growing better today!

Gallery

Quick lesson! Seed your rows a few weeks apart to ensure a continual harvest throughout the season, this way you won't have everything ready at once and don't have gaps in harvesting. You can see the arugula here that's sprouting at different stages.

Seed saving! Beans are a very simple way to get started on seed saving to plant for the following year. They self pollinate so you can save the seed from the parent plant to grow the same bean the following year. Plants like squash or cucumber cross polinate so if you have one or two variety's growing they will cross and you won't get the same fruit / vegetables next year.

Chive blossoms! You can put these flowers in vinegar and make chive blossom vinegar or you can pull them apart and sprinkle them on salads or pasta or just eat handfuls of them as if they're candy like the kids at the school garden.

Vancouver citrus from a rooftop garden! These sudachi are a hybrid of yuzu and mardarins and are hardy to -8.
